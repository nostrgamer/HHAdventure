One of the steps that I was most intimidated by when starting the van build was electrical. All the posts and videos I'd seen looked highly technical, and I knew virtually nothing about electricity. I spent a lot of time researching this part of the build because I lacked confidence in this area. Several iterations of what I wanted to build had to be drafted and rejected before I got to my final wire-up. Finally, after going back and forth, and after some great advice from the father of my sister's husband, Darrell, aka G-pa, who knows a lot about electrical, I was able to come up with this design.

![Electrical design](/images/electrical/IMG_4592.jpg)

After getting the design completed, it was time to remove the bed from the van, which will be reinstalled later and aligned with the new Transit capsules. Then I started drilling holes in the van, my favorite thing, obviously not. But seriously, many holes later, progress was being made.

![Drilling for wire looms](/images/electrical/IMG_4602.jpg)

![The finished hole with an extra mistake hole](/images/electrical/IMG_4603.jpg)

![More holes for the DC/DC](/images/electrical/IMG_4616.jpg)

After making a lot of holes, it was time to start pulling out the wire loom and laying down the various gauges of wire along the runs.

![Laying out the wire loom and wire on the bench](/images/electrical/IMG_4604.jpg)

Then I began the process of pulling the loom through the van cavities to where it needed to go. I thought this part would be very hard, but in fact it was mostly just a bit of physical labor. The hard thinking was done by then. One thing I had to be sure of was labeling everything, hence the green tape everywhere at the ends of each run.

![Wire crossing the ceiling to the kitchen](/images/electrical/IMG_4633.jpg)

Overall, after much consternation, I really enjoyed wiring up the van. While I'm not totally done, we still need to wire the DC panel into the Yeti 1500, the part that worried me most is complete. I'm looking forward to the next steps, where we get the walls ready and this starts to look a bit more finished.
